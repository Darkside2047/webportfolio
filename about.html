Short Introduction

Motivation for Web Development

In my early years I’ve always had an interest in astronomy, technology and being creative. As I grew older, computers and coding caught my eye. Career interests ranged from wanting to become an engineer, to pursuing computer science. I started my college career at North Carolina A & T University. Midway through, I enlisted in the United States Air Force for six years as security forces. I had an opportunity to travel the world and despite some of the bad times, I absolutely loved it! After my six-year contract was finished, I decided to pursue an associate’s degree in web development at Wake Technical Community College.

Traveling Experience

The most enjoyable aspect of my military enlistment was traveling around the world. I truly enjoyed the experience of immersing myself in other cultures, learning new languages, and enjoying their cuisine. My key take away from this experience was the opportunity for growth. Constantly being in an unfamiliar environment where I wasn’t 100% fluent in the native language whether I was on official duties, or traveling for vacation gave me a tremendous opportunity to see how other societies collaborate to achieve their goals. It is my dream to bring forth that drive for teamwork, and cohesiveness in the field of web development to create innovative applications and push humanity forward!
